Solomon's Seal



Scientific Name: Polygonatum

Information:

[1] A woodland plant that grows best in partial shade and enjoys a moist but well-drained soil. The plant's ornamental architectural foliage contrasts well with other plants in a mixed border. Plant out from late autumn to early spring. Site in a partially shaded position, with well-drained soil that is rich in organic matter. Needs very little care and attention once established. Ideal for shaded flower beds and borders or as under planting for tall shrubs. The stems are excellent as Cut Flowers.

Dan's Notes

This one is interesting. The cut roots supposedly look like Hebrew characters, hence the name. Polygonatum comes from the ancient Greek for "many knees", as its rhizome (stem) has multiple joints. Also, the roots "bear depressions that resemble royal seals" which is another reason for the name "Solomon's Seal". There are tons of species of this flower. Many are used as ornamental plants, and they have uses in the treatment of diabetes and hyperglycemia. It is used as an aphrodisiac, and can be used to treat pain, fever, inflammation, allergy, and weakness. Many species have been used in China for a long time. They have been cooked or used raw and served as a side dish with meat and rice.
